Structural week. I folded most of my projects into one monorepo, moved the journal onto Vercel, and rolled a shared design system across every web app. Brief also picked up a third case.

## monday

Brief A+ pass. Priority cards, a sorted checklist, Harding and Chantler promoted to the top. Marked Arvay Finlay declined after Robin Gage passed on the 25th. Tally moved the PWD deadline to the June 18 extension date. Added TLS inspection detection so the school app shows a real error on locked-down WiFi instead of failing silently. Expanded the portfolio reading list to 80 ranked books.

## tuesday

Brief got a full v6 redesign, a clean single-case layout. Raised the RCMP damages ceiling to four or five million with new comparables. Activated the third case with a municipal liability and section 285 countdown, officer name held pending ATIP. A slim print mode took the brief PDF from thirty pages down to seven. Pulled in the Surrey Memorial records and updated the evidence checklist.

## wednesday

Shipped the CFA palette across every web app, cream, red, yellow, charcoal. NYC got sound effects on macOS, haptics on iOS, and difficulty levels on web. Wiretext shipped PNG export and component search on all three platforms. Added Supabase auth and moved my profile onto a real account. Security pass too: pulled a tracked Vercel token, hardened the session cookie, fixed some CORS. App icons landed on every iOS app.

## thursday

The big one. Folded school, cadence, epiphany, and bcgd into the apps monorepo, then moved the journal onto Vercel from inside it. Brief shipped v6.2.0 with an auto-deploy hook and resilient init. Renamed the macOS journal app and fixed a half-rendered icon.

## friday

The longest day of the week. Tally got a real internationalization pass, a translation pipeline plus a locale runtime, shipped as v3.4.0. Epiphany got the most attention: I moved the stock data off the retired endpoint onto the FMP stable API everywhere, web plus the iOS and macOS clients, brought the blob cache back, got the test suite green again, and refreshed the whitepaper. Started wiring a SnapTrade broker sync so the holdings can come from a real account instead of being typed in.

Brief kept moving. The third case got its web port, I reconciled the first case across web, iOS, and macOS, propagated the section 19 limitation, and pulled the damages ceiling back to a defensible two to three million from the looser number earlier in the week. Brief now auto-deploys on every change.

School got a polish pass. True white light mode across every page, a device-auto theme that follows the system, a low-ink print mode, and JSON schema validation on the grades and quizzes data so a bad edit fails loudly instead of silently. NYC now starts paused so the player drives from the first frame. The portfolio picked up a library checkout tracker with a due-date countdown. Captured the PDF task lists into the project roadmaps and redacted some PII out of a tally fixture along the way.
